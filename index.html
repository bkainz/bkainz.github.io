Bernhard Kainz

I am a Professor at Friedrich-Alexander-University Erlangen-Nuremberg, heading the Image Data Exploration and Analysis Lab (IDEA Lab), and in the Department of Computing at Imperial College London, where I lead the human-in-the-loop computing group and co-lead BioMedIA. We are a post-pandemic, borderless research group spanning nations and institutions.

Our research focuses on advancing intelligent algorithms for multi-modal healthcare. We develop generative and discriminative machine learning methods to enhance diagnostic decision-making, provide real-time guidance during diagnostic procedures, and explore normative learning to ensure the safety of ML at the front lines of patient care.

Current Research Questions

Can we democratize rare healthcare expertise through Machine Learning, providing guidance in real-time applications and second-reader expertise in retrospective analysis?
Can we develop normative learning from large populations — integrating imaging, patient records, and -omics — leading to data analysis that mimics human decision making?
Can we provide human interpretability and effective human-machine teamwork to support the right for explanation in healthcare AI decisions?
