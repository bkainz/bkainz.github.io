Bernhard Kainz

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Computing at Imperial College London. I am researching translational high-performance medical data analysis and interactive real-time image processing techniques. I am working with the BioMedIA group and collaborate intensively with King's College London, Division of Imaging Sciences and Biomedical Engineering, St. Thomas Hospital London.
In 2013 I moved to Imperial College London with a EU FP7 Marie-Curie Fellowship, working together with Prof. Daniel Rueckert and Prof. Jo Hajnal (KCL). Since then I am supporting the EPSRC/Wellcome trust project iFind with high-performance computing methods.
I finished my Ph.D.-Thesis within the field of medical visualization, volume graphics and GPU algorithms in the team of Prof. Dieter Schmalstieg at the Institute for Computer Graphics and Vision at Graz University of Technology , Austria in 2011. My research interests are the interconnection of medical image data acquisition, fast hardware accelerated medical image processing and interactive real-time visualization.
